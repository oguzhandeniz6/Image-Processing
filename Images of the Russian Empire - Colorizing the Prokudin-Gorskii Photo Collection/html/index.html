In this problem, we need to combine the RGB channels of a photo. But while doing this, we notice that the RGB channels of the photo are not fully aligned. So we need to align these channels first by using some methods. Then we use some enhancement methods over the photo we have obtained and ensure that an image is close to the original.





First of all, I needed to seperate channels. After that, I had 3 different, equal-sized photos. I knew I need to align these but I didn't know how to do it. I did a lot of research on the internet and came across 2 methods:
The 1st method, the SSD method (Sum of Squared Differences), seems easier and faster in practice, but unfortunately the results did not satisfy me.
Then I tried the 2nd method NCC (Normalized Cross Correlation) and the results I got were quite satisfactory.
I decided to use this method even though it works slower than SSD. If the photos we were working on were much larger sized photos, I would have optimized NCC using the Image Pyramid method, but they were all small sized photos so I didn't need any optimization.
Although the results I got after alignment were satisfactory, I was aware that it could still be improved. So first I applied the Histogram Equalization method to the raw image. With that alone, there was a noticeable difference. Then I played with the Gamma settings of the result photos and tried to improve the photo even more by using the Laplacian method.
I also tried the Gaussian, Average, and Median Filtering methods, but since none of them produced a satisfactory result, I decided not to use them.


Photos in order:
1- Non-Aligned Image
2- Aligned But Non-Histogram Equalization
3- Aligned with Histogram Equalization
4- Aligned with Histogram Equalization and Gamma Adjustment
5- Aligned with Histogram Equalization, Gamma Adjustment and Laplacian Method
